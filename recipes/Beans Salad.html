Beans Salad Recipe

Beans Salad

This enhanced version of the colorful salad incorporates a variety of beans along with edamame and chickpeas, creating a protein-rich and satisfying dish. With the addition of beans, this salad becomes even heartier and more nutritious, making it an excellent option for a complete meal or a substantial side dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup frozen edamame, thawed
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas (garbanzo beans), drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 11/2 cup red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions

Step 1

In a large mixing bowl, combine the thawed edamame, drained chickpeas, drained black beans, and corn kernels. Add the diced red bell pepper, diced cucumber, finely chopped red onion, and fresh cilantro to the bowl with the beans, edamame, and chickpeas. Gently toss the ingredients to create a balanced mixture.

Step 2

In a separate small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, honey or maple syrup, minced garlic, ground cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined. Drizzle the tangy dressing over the bean, edamame, and vegetable mixture. Gently toss the salad to ensure all the ingredients are coated with the dressing.

Step 3

Once the edamame, chickpea, and bean salad is well-mixed and dressed, it's ready to be served. Allow the salad to rest for a short while before serving to allow the flavors to meld together. Serve the salad as a hearty and nutritious meal on its own, or pair it with whole grains for added sustenance.
